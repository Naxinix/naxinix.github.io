Overview

First of all... what is Sokoban?

  • The game is played on a board of squares, where each square is floor or a wall. Some squares on the floor contain boxes and some squares on the floor are marked as storage locations.
  • The player is confined to the board and can move horizontally or vertically over empty squares (never through walls or boxes).
  • The player can move a box by walking towards it and pushing it towards the box beyond. You can't pull the boxes and you can't push them to squares with walls or other boxes.
  • The number of boxes equals the number of storage locations. The puzzle is solved when all boxes are placed in their storage places.

Whith this premises, I aimed to create 6 Sokoban levels following 6 different methodologies, which I will develop furter down below.

Level 1 - "Trial and error" Methodology

First, we have the Trial and error Methodology. In summary, its process is the following:
Set goal areas → Then surround freely boxes around the goals.
Place walls close to the goals→ Make sure at all times that the challenge can be solved.
Now we refine the level → Reduce superfluous spaces , brings the game elements closer.

Level 2 - "Goal-based flow" Methodology

Set goal areas→ In a way that it can be solved in just one go.

Place the walls to increase difficulty → That will make it difficult for the player to make that move despite seeing the goal or being really close to it.

Level 3 - "Parking" Methodology

We set areas on the level that serve to park and redirect extra boxes → This can be done thanks to the stage layout or to a great number of boxes that allows us to fill the level to our liking.

Level 4 - "Alternative Loop" Methodology

Similar to the goal-based method, but this time an extra box can must be moved towards the goal first in order to open the way to the real box.

Level 5 - "Dummy" Methodology

The Dummy Methodology characterizes for having a fairly easy solution, but the trick is that we decorate the level to make the player doubt about every move they make.

This methodology, followed by the next one, become a really powerful combo.

Level 6 - "Trapper" Methodology

Last but not least, the Trapper Methodology consists in arranging the boxes and obstacles to make the player think there is an easy solution. However, the player is not led to the solution but instead to a not so obvious trap. To make the trick work, it's a good idea to superfluous space to make believe you can maneuver but you actually can't.


Notice how pushing the nearest box too early makes the whole level unbeatable.
